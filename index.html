Verifiable Financial Documents

DOC.USD converts invoices, receipts, and financial records into cryptographically verifiable instruments that can be audited independently. Content is addressed by hash, stored on IPFS, and resolvable through public registry mappings and on-chain anchors.

What DOC.USD Solves

Problem
Financial documents are files that require trust, not instruments that can be verified.
Impact
Fraud, waste, and audit delays increase when records cannot be validated independently.
Fix
DOC.USD makes records verifiable by default through deterministic hashing and public resolution.

How It Works

DOC.USD → INSTABYTE → INSTA-FICHE → IPFS → Public Registry → On-Chain Anchor

Every artifact can be reproduced, hashed, and verified against its public pointers. The registry binds human-readable names to immutable content IDs (CIDs), with optional pointer files and SHA-256 digests.
